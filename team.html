Founder & President

Dr. Sean Casey

Over two decades as a NASA astrophysicist and airborne astronomer — combined with deep expertise in commercial space business development — shaped a simple conviction: the best way to build the next generation of space infrastructure is to build it with students.

Dr. Casey founded the San Diego Space Institute to close the gap between university engineering programs and the applied, systems-level work that aerospace employers actually need. Through SDSI's flagship program Sentinel-Bio+, students at SDSU and SJSU follow NASA design review processes, build real flight hardware, and develop the documented experience that sets them apart in a competitive field.

Prior to founding SDSI, Dr. Casey co-founded the Silicon Valley Space Center and served as VP of Commercial Business Development at Atlas Space Operations — bringing both the technical depth of a research scientist and the commercial acumen of a business executive to every program he leads.

His academic credentials — a Ph.D. in Astrophysics from the University of Chicago and dual MBAs from the Berkeley Haas and Columbia Business Schools — reflect the same boundary-crossing that defines SDSI's approach: rigorous science, real engineering, and a clear-eyed understanding of where the industry is going.
