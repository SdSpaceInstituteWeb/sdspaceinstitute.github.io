March 15, 2026

SDSI Recognized by IRS as 501(c)(3) Public Charity

The IRS formally recognized SDSI as a tax-exempt public charity under Section 501(c)(3), effective April 10, 2025. The determination confirms SDSI's eligibility to receive tax-deductible contributions and supports continued expansion of programs in aerospace education, technology development, and workforce training. Donations to SDSI are deductible under Section 170 of the Internal Revenue Code.

November 24, 2025

SDSI Launches San Diego Space Business Roundtable

SDSI established the San Diego Space Business Roundtable, a quarterly forum for leaders across San Diego's space, aerospace, defense, AI, and Blue Tech sectors. The forum meets four times per year in a hybrid format. The inaugural session was held in January 2026. Organizations and professionals whose work intersects with space or aerospace are welcome to participate.

October 14, 2025

NASA California Space Grant Selection

SDSI, SJSU, and SDSU were selected by the NASA California Space Grant Consortium under NASA's 2025 Workforce Development Program. Two awards support Sentinel-Bio+: one for spacecraft bus development at SJSU and one for payload integration at SDSU. Both teams work under SDSI's nonprofit coordination and industry mentorship.

November 20, 2025

SDSI Attends UC San Diego Space Day Forum

SDSI President Dr. Sean Casey and Board Director Dave Strobel attended UC San Diego's Space Day Forum, engaging with representatives from DARPA, SpaceWERX, Viasat, New Vista Capital, and UC San Diego research teams. Discussions covered partnerships in space-weather science, workforce development, and small-satellite programs.

December 24, 2025

Sentinel-Bio+ Internal Design Review Complete

SDSI completed an internal Preliminary Design Review for Sentinel-Bio+, covering satellite architecture, payload design, and spacecraft bus. No blocking deficiencies were identified. The program cleared to proceed toward an external PDR planned for early 2026, which will determine readiness for detailed design and hardware procurement.

Program Update
September 11, 2025

Sentinel-Bio+ Program Launched

SDSI launched Sentinel-Bio+ with NASA California Space Grant seed funding, partnering with SJSU and SDSU to train the next generation of aerospace engineers through a hands-on CubeSat program tied to NASA's human spaceflight and heliophysics priorities. Student teams began engineering training and prototype development in late 2025.
